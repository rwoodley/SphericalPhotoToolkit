Transformations:

Möbius transformations:

  • Set Fixed Point 1 at center of viewport. When debugging, this point is represented by a red dot on the image.
  • Set Fixed Point 2 at center of viewport. When debugging, this point is represented by a blue dot on the image.
  • Rotate left around fixed points.(*)
  • Rotate right around fixed points.(*)
  • Stop rotating.
  • No rotation.
  • Do conformal zoom in on point 1, out on point 2. (*)
  • Do conformal zoom out on point 1, in on point 2(*).
  • Cancel zoom.
  • Allows you to a loxodromic transformation. It selects the complex point at the center of the viewport to be the scaling factor. If this is a real number (which it is by default), this corresponds to a simple zoom. A complex number is a combination of rotation and zoom. When debugging, this point is represented by a yellow dot on the image.

    • (*) If you click on rotate or zoom without having first selected the fixed points, the system will set the current center of the viewport to be fixed point 1, and the antipodal point to be fixed point 2.

    Complex transformations:

    If selected, these are applied after the Möbius transformations have been applied. So 'x' in the equations below is understood to be a complex number that is the result of any selected Möbius transformations. If more than one of the following transforms is selected they are applied in turn taking the output of the previous transformation to be the input ('x') of the new one.

    • Miscellaneous other functions:

  • Turn off all transforms, revert to original image/video.
  • Toggle view to see effect of transformations on the rectangular texture.
  • Toggle Show/hide dots representing fixed points.

    • Video Controls:

    There are only visible if you are playing a video.
  • Skip backwards in the video.
  • Pause the video.
  • Unpause the video.
  • Skip forwards in the video.
  • Replay the video.

    • Camera Controls:

  • Rotate camera to left. Successive clicks increase the speed.
  • Rotate camera up. Stops at the zenith.
  • Rotate camera down. Stops at the nadir.
  • Rotate camera to the right.
  • Stop rotating the camera.
  • Flip camera around to view the antipodal point opposite the current position.
  • Decrease the field of view by 15%.
  • Increase the field of view by 15%.
